Artificial Intelligence (AI) Powered Traffic Apprehension

By: Shealtiel Aguas and Eithan Serrano

What is the dilemma?

AI Systems, namely ALPR (automatic license plate recognition), Computer Vision, automated violation classification, and more, can detect violations and issue tickets, which initially aims to promise faster enforcement, fewer bribery opportunities, and improve road safety. However, heavy reliance on AI Traffic Apprehension raises concerns about the violation’s accuracy, biased apprehension, opaque decision processes, misuse of surveillance (mass surveillance), and due-process problems for the automated notices and violations. The implementation of this forces the society to choose between efficiency and safety gains, protecting rights, fairness, and trust.

Choices

  • Automate ticketing, leaving minimal reviews to the traffic/city officials.
  • Semi-automate the ticketing system. Only use AI apprehension for detection, and require human review before issuance of tickets.
  • Reject the implementation of AI Traffic Apprehension at all.

Compromises per Choice

Choice 1 (Ticketing Automation):

  • Advantages:Broader coverage, consistent enforcement, lesser in-person contact, corruption opportunities are mitigated, and potential safety gains.
  • Disadvantages: Higher risk of wrongful fines and algorithm errors.

Choice 2 (Semi-Automated Ticketing):

  • Advantages:Balances efficiency and fairness with human interference, enables auditing, limits data use, and higher public trust.
  • Disadvantages: Ticket processing and issuance is slower, and requires higher implementation costs.

Choice 3 (Reject Automation):

  • Advantages:Stronger protection of individual liberties with protected due process, avoids algorithmic bias.
  • Disadvantages:Fewer enforcement resources, and has more opportunities for corrupt interactions between the traffic enforcers and the drivers.
