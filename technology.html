Mental Health Impact of Technology

By: John Dave Apostol

What is the dilemma?

The ethical dilemma regarding technology’s impact on mental health arises from the tension between the benefits of digital tools and the risks of excessive or unmindful use. While technology provides support systems, educational resources, and social connections, it can also contribute to anxiety, depression, sleep disturbances, cognitive issues, social isolation, and reduced self-esteem. Users, developers, and policymakers must balance promoting access to helpful technology with preventing its potential harms.

Choices

  • Choice 1: Encourage unrestricted use — Allow users full access to digital tools, platforms, and apps without limitations.
  • Choice 2: Implement usage guidelines and moderation — Provide limits, notifications, or structured use of technology to reduce harm.
  • Choice 3: Restrict or monitor technology usage — Limit access to certain apps, content, or screen time to safeguard mental health.

Compromises per Choice

Choice 1 (Unrestricted use):

  • Advantages: Full access to benefits of technology, including support networks and educational tools.
  • Disadvantages: Increased risk of anxiety, depression, sleep disturbances, social isolation, and other mental health issues.

Choice 2 (Guidelines and moderation):

  • Advantages: Balances access and protection; users can benefit from technology while minimizing harm.
  • Disadvantages: Requires effort to educate and enforce; some users may ignore guidelines.

Choice 3 (Restriction and monitoring):

  • Advantages: Protects users from mental health risks and harmful overuse.
  • Disadvantages: Limits access to beneficial resources and social connections; may be perceived as restrictive or paternalistic.
